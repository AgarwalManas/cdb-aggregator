# Aiming Your Portfolio Project at Wealthsimple: What Leadership Wants in 2026

## TL;DR
- **Your instinct is right, with a sharpening.** Wealthsimple's leadership is explicitly betting that Canada's Consumer-Driven Banking (open banking) + Real-Time Rail will let it become the "financial operating system" for Canadians, and the company states plainly that lack of access to data is one of its biggest product constraints — so an FDX-first account aggregator with a real consent layer hits a genuine strategic nerve. But the single most resonant version of that project leans hard into **consent management, traceability, and replacing screen-scraping**, ideally with a nod to agentic-AI delegation, because that is exactly the frontier their own people are working on.
- **The four things leadership is most excited about for 2026:** (1) deepening the platform so clients "bring their entire financial life" (households, business banking, kids/teens, USD borderless accounts); (2) scaling advice "through both humans and AI," including an AI investment-research dashboard from their Fey acquisition; (3) advanced/sophisticated investing (options, direct indexing, private markets); and (4) winning the regulatory-competition fight (transfer-fee bans, open banking, RTR) on the road to a future IPO.
- **Engineering culture to mirror in your build:** "maker-owner," "keep it simple," ship fast (100+ deploys/day), AI-native development (Cursor/Copilot, internal LLM gateway), microservices in Ruby on Rails + Java/Kotlin with React/React Native, and a relentless client-first, low-fee framing. Demonstrate judgment and ownership, not just code.

## Key Findings

**1. The "financial operating system" thesis is the through-line.** Across 2025–2026, Wealthsimple's two flagship "Wealthsimple Presents" showcases ("End of Banking," June 2025; "For Nerds Only," October 2025) and the May 21, 2026 Calgary event ("Wealthsimple Takes Over Your Life") all push one idea: become the single place a Canadian household manages everything. CPO and co-founder Brett Huneycutt described a future where the app "negotiates rates for you, builds and adjusts your portfolios as your life situation changes, minimizes your taxes, makes payments, [and] transfers money" — and, per Environics' recap of the June 2025 event, "a truly intelligent financial assistant that works for you in the background." This is the strategic frame any portfolio project should speak to.

**2. Open banking is framed as both a cause they champion and a constraint they feel.** Wealthsimple has been one of the loudest fintech advocates for Canada's Consumer-Driven Banking framework. Its own magazine essay states plainly: "lack of access to data is one of Wealthsimple's biggest hindrances when it comes to making better products, and making them available to everyone." Its 2019 federal open-banking submission documented that its Roundup feature suffered an average of 10 failed bank connections per day because it relied on third-party screen-scraping aggregators — a concrete pain point open banking is meant to solve. Today Wealthsimple still depends on Flinks and Plaid (plus its in-house "Wealthsimple Connection Tool") for external bank links — exactly the screen-scraping/credential-based plumbing that FDX-based open banking is designed to replace.

**3. They have named people doing exactly the work your project simulates.** Abdi Hersi, Wealthsimple's Director of Payments Strategy & Partnership Development, represents Wealthsimple on the **FDX Canada Working Group** and sits on the advisory boards of Open Banking Expo and Open Finance Canada. Hanna Zaidi, VP Payments Strategy & Chief Compliance Officer, sits on the Bank of Canada's Consumer-Driven Banking Advisory Committee and was a founding board member of Open Banking Initiative Canada. (Note: an Open Banking Expo bio refers to "Abdi" — his surname is Hersi, not Mohamed.) This means a credible FDX-first build is legible to specific senior people there — and reflects work they personally value.

**4. Consent, trust labels, and traceability are the live frontier.** At Open Banking Expo Canada (article dated May 4, 2026), Hersi stressed trust and accreditation: "Trust is a key factor, especially when you're dealing with people's personal financial information. One thing that we've been advocating for is a label that financial institutions can use… so clients can quickly identify, 'this is a trusted institution, they've gone through the full accreditation process. I'm willing to engage in Open Banking use cases through their portals.'" Separately, FDX launched an Agentic AI initiative (Call for Input, April 14, 2026) focused on agent identity, consent and delegation, data minimization, downstream accountability, and traceability — signaling where data-sharing standards are heading. (No Wealthsimple individual is publicly named on that task force.)

**5. AI is a first-class priority — in both product and engineering.** CEO Michael Katchen's December 2025 client letter: "We'll scale financial advice through both humans and AI." Wealthsimple acquired Montréal's Fey to build an AI-powered investment research/trading dashboard. On the engineering side, their published "Engineering AI Strategy" says "AI isn't just a productivity boost — it's an inflection point for software engineering," and states verbatim: "Our LLM Gateway serves 500 internal users daily… Across Engineering, 85% of our developers use GitHub Copilot, and we recently onboarded half our developers to the Cursor code editor." Their culture manual now states building with AI is "an operating expectation… It's not optional."

## Details

### What leadership is prioritizing for 2026 and beyond

**Platform depth / "bring your whole financial life":** Katchen's year-end 2025 letter and the May 2026 Calgary launch make clear the 2026 roadmap is about completeness:
- **Wealthsimple Households** (live): a consolidated view of all accounts — chequing, investments, mortgages, group RRSPs — whether held at Wealthsimple *or elsewhere*. This is effectively a first-party account aggregator and is the most direct adjacency to your project.
- **Business banking** (rolling out 2026): business chequing (rates up to 2.25%), prepaid business card, USD business accounts, business portfolio line of credit.
- **Kids/teen accounts** (fall 2026) and **trade-on-behalf-of-family** (summer 2026) — explicitly motivated by the insecurity of password-sharing, per Senior Director of Product (Banking) Danish Ajmeri: today families "navigate all sorts of really insecure ways of doing that, whether that's sharing their passwords or trying to call in and pretend to be your father or mother." (Consent/delegation theme again.)
- **USD "borderless" chequing** (fall 2026): single account spanning US and Canadian payment rails, described as the first of its kind in Canada.
- **Spend insights** tooling gaining subscription identification and "investment surplus" alerts.

Ajmeri's framing of the platform logic: "The products are designed to work together. A client who banks with us is more likely to invest, borrow, and bring their household or business on." On IPO, he kept it narrow: "While an IPO isn't on our immediate roadmap, we are growing rapidly – so our current focus is on the product roadmap and the four million clients we serve today." Katchen has separately said going public is "very much in the cards" but not a current focus; he wants "to welcome our clients as shareholders in building the future."

**Scale context (why their confidence is well-founded):** Wealthsimple crossed **C$100 billion in AUA on October 22, 2025 — three years ahead of its 2023 five-year target** — having doubled from $50B in a single year, and reported ~$125B AUA across ~4 million clients by March 31, 2026. Its October 2025 Series F raised up to $750M CAD (co-led by Dragoneer and GIC, with new investor CPP Investments) at a **$10-billion CAD post-money valuation**, double its valuation a year earlier. Per Katchen (Financial Post, Dec 2025): "Wealthsimple is now among the top five institutions in the country for net deposits. Last year, we did about $20 billion or so in net deposits. This year, we'll do about $40 billion. That probably puts us in the top three… we've opened 35 per cent of all (First Home Savings Accounts) since the investment account was introduced a couple years ago."

**Advanced investing:** At "For Nerds Only" (Oct 2025), CCO Paul Teshima framed three fundamentals — low fees, diversification, leverage — and the firm shipped/teased zero-commission options, cheaper crypto, gold, direct indexing, a private-markets "Summit" portfolio, Retirement Accelerator RRSP loans, Norbert's Gambit (early 2026), and the Fey-based AI research dashboard. Teshima: "We believe that we need to continue to innovate in the world of investing, and AI is going to be part of that," with the caveat that tools must "pass rigorous testing for accuracy and have the right safeguards in place."

**The regulatory-competition fight:** Katchen (Financial Post, Dec 2025) is optimistic that "the pieces are now in place to create the most competitive financial products Canadians have seen in decades," citing the budget's investment-transfer-fee ban, open banking, and Real-Time Rail. He noted 2026 is when transfer rules "actually have to be implemented" and that Wealthsimple will keep "partnering with various stakeholders, the government and regulators, on what that implementation looks like."

### Stance on Consumer-Driven Banking and FDX (most important for your project)

- **Position:** unequivocally pro-open-banking. Hanna Zaidi compares it to phone-number portability; the company's essay argues open banking "isn't any less secure. It would, in fact, drive banks and financial companies to upgrade to newer, more secure technology."
- **They feel the screen-scraping pain directly** (the Roundup ~10 daily connection failures; current Flinks/Plaid dependence for external linking and KYC/AML at onboarding).
- **They are at the FDX table** via Abdi Hersi (FDX Canada Working Group), and on the Bank of Canada advisory committee via Hanna Zaidi.
- **Regulatory reality / timing:** Canada's framework was restructured in Budget 2025 — **Bill C-15 (Royal Assent March 26, 2026) repealed the original Consumer-Driven Banking Act and replaced it with a comprehensive new CDBA**, moving oversight from FCAC to the **Bank of Canada** (building on its Retail Payment Activities Act role, under which it already supervises Wealthsimple, Koho, Brim, and Venn). Phase 1 (read access) was targeted for early 2026; Phase 2 (write access — payment initiation, account switching) targeted mid-2027, contingent on Real-Time Rail. As of early 2026 the Bank of Canada had not committed to a firm launch date (its payments head said it was "premature" to set one), so a 2026 launch is widely seen as at risk. **Implication for you:** building FDX-first *now* demonstrates exactly the forward-looking judgment they reward, and positions your project at the read-access frontier with a credible write-access/payment-initiation roadmap.
- FDX is the consensus North American standard (OAuth 2.0 + FAPI security profiles; five core principles: Control, Access, Transparency, Traceability, Security; royalty-free to implement). An FDX-aligned consent layer with token-based (not credential) access, granular/time-limited permissions, and an auditable consent/revocation log is precisely the "right" architecture, and directly answers the screen-scraping problems Wealthsimple itself has documented.

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 — Build the FDX-first aggregator, but make the consent layer the star (next 4–6 weeks).**
- Implement against the **FDX API data model** (accounts, balances, transactions, investment holdings) using **OAuth 2.0 + FAPI** patterns. Even if you mock the data provider, structure it as if the bank is an FDX-compliant data provider so the design reads as "open-banking-native," not "another Plaid wrapper."
- Make the **consent layer** auditable and first-class: granular scopes, time-limited grants, a visible consent dashboard, one-tap revocation, and an immutable **consent/traceability log**. This maps directly to FDX's five principles (Control, Access, Transparency, Traceability, Security) and to Hersi's "trusted institution / accreditation label" theme.
- Explicitly contrast your token-based approach with **screen-scraping** in the README — cite the security and reliability problems (credential storage, breakage on UI changes) Wealthsimple itself has lived with through Roundup and Flinks/Plaid.

**Stage 2 — Add a differentiator that signals where they're going (weeks 6–10). Pick ONE:**
- **Agentic delegation / "intent" layer:** let a user delegate a scoped, revocable, fully logged task to an AI agent (e.g., "find idle cash earning <1% and suggest a move"). This lands on FDX's April 2026 Agentic-AI focus areas (agent identity, consent delegation, data minimization, downstream accountability, traceability) and Huneycutt's "intelligent assistant in the background" vision — without overreaching on write access.
- **Household net-worth aggregation** mirroring "Wealthsimple Households": consolidate external accounts into one net-worth view with surplus/idle-cash insights. This is literally a feature they just shipped, so you'd be speaking their language.
- **"Investment surplus" / fee-leak detector:** analyze aggregated transactions to surface wasted fees or cash that should be invested — echoing their spend-insights roadmap and low-fee gospel.

**Stage 3 — Package it the way Wealthsimple packages things (weeks 10–12).**
- Mirror their **engineering values** in the artifact itself: keep it simple, write clean human-friendly (and AI-friendly) code, include real automated tests, and document architecture decisions like a maker-owner. Build with AI tooling and *say so* — it's an explicit operating expectation there.
- Use their **stack** where reasonable (a Rails or Kotlin service + React/React Native client) to reduce the gap between "your project" and "their codebase."
- Write a crisp **README framed around the client and the regulatory moment**: the problem (screen-scraping, fragmented finances), the CDBA/FDX context with dates, your consent architecture, and the trade-offs you chose. Prepare a tight 30-minute "Project Review" walkthrough — that is an actual stage in their engineering interview.

**Outreach:** Target the payments/open-banking and Financial-Operating-System orgs. The named, reachable advocates are Abdi Hersi (payments strategy / FDX) and the FOS/data-platform engineering leaders; engaging thoughtfully with their public talks (Open Banking Expo, BetaKit podcast, the engineering blog) before reaching out will land better than a cold résumé.

**Benchmarks that would change this plan:**
- If the **Bank of Canada commits to a firm Phase-1 launch date** (read access), pivot the demo from "future-looking prototype" toward "ready-to-integrate against real FDX endpoints," and emphasize accreditation-readiness.
- If **Wealthsimple ships its own external-bank *write* features** (payments/switching) before you finish, shift your differentiator away from generic aggregation toward the **agentic-delegation/consent-governance** angle, which is less commoditized.
- If a **Wealthsimple engineering role specifically referencing open banking / FDX / data connectivity** opens, tailor the project's README and your application to that role's language.

## Caveats
- **Some 2026 product items are announced, not shipped.** Kids/teen accounts, USD borderless chequing, and trade-on-behalf are dated to summer/fall 2026; treat them as roadmap, not live. Wealthsimple has had public stumbles (credit-card launch delays, a crypto-fee increase reversed within days, and a late-2025 security breach exposing some client data including SINs) — so demonstrating *security/consent rigor* is doubly resonant.
- **Open-banking timing is genuinely uncertain.** Phase-1 read access was targeted for early 2026 but the Bank of Canada had not set a firm date as of early 2026; write access/payment initiation hinges on Real-Time Rail (targeted mid-2027). Build FDX-first, but don't claim live regulatory integration.
- **No Wealthsimple individual is publicly confirmed on the FDX Agentic AI Task Force**, and I found no direct quote from a Wealthsimple person specifically about AI-agent "intent/traceability"; that framing comes from FDX (institutional, via CEO Kevin Feltes) and the FIDO Alliance. Treat the agentic angle as well-aligned-but-inferential, not something they've explicitly asked builders for.
- **LinkedIn/X post specifics are thin.** Katchen's @mkatchen account is active but his most-engaged recent posts mix company milestones, Canadian-competitiveness/productivity themes, and personal posts; treat "what he reposts/champions" as directional (pro-competition, pro-Canadian-innovation, pro-crypto-regulatory-clarity) rather than a precise product signal.
- **Power Corporation context:** Wealthsimple is majority-owned (~52–54%) by Power Corp entities (Power Financial, IGM Financial, and Sagard's Portage Ventures). Power valued its stake at roughly $3.8B as of March 31, 2026; valuations across the fintech sector have been under some pressure, and Power kept its Wealthsimple valuation flat in at least one recent quarter despite asset growth — relevant background if you discuss IPO timing, which leadership keeps deliberately vague.
